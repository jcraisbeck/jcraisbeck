Mathematical Reinforcement Learning

My research topic is mathematical reinforcement learning, which at time of writing does not seem to have much of a definition, or a following. That may be because I have only affixed the word "mathematical" to the subject "reinforcement learning" as a way to placate my desire for rigor, but I do not think so. By mathematical reinforcement learning I mean an account of reinforcement learning which focuses on a formal understanding of the structures which already exist in Reinforcement Learning and in [discrete-time] decision processes.

I contend that, contrary to the trends present in Reinforcement Learning as a whole, formal mathematical definitions of decision processes are necessary for solid progress to be made in Reinforcement Learning, on problems of practical and theoretical importance. I hope that soon the fruits of the investigations resulting from those definitions will show the necessity of the definitions and the value of approaching Reinforcement Learning problems from a mathematical perspective, as the originator of our field, Richard Bellman did when he first approached the "class of mathematical problems which arise in connection with situations which require that a bounded or unbounded sequence of operations be performed for the purpose of achieving a desired result." which he later called "multistage decision processes".
