Basic Beliefs

  • The material is not knowable without the immaterial.
  • The immaterial is [in part] knowable without the material.
  • Mathematical truths are more real than physical objects.
  • Consciousness is not material.
  • Determinism is incorrect.
  • Materialism is incorrect.
  • There are moral truths.
  • Morality is not material.
  • God exists.
  • There is exactly one God.
  • God created all that there is.
  • People are made in the image of God.
  • People have greater moral standing than animals.
  • The immaterial is well-experienced on mountains.
  • In the final account, all belongs not to man or to men, but to God.
  • Bodily autonomy is not an intrinsic moral good.
  • Temporal autonomy is an intrinsic moral good.
  • The basis of morality is not material.
  • Science is a method, not a purpose.

      • In committing these beliefs to appear in public, I hope sincerely that the reader in the present era is able to see that I am making myself liable in the public sphere for the affirmative answer to the question "Are you willing to be damned for the greater glory of God?".

      In my view, the pursuit of material explanations, because they are broadly accepted, has hollowed among academics the desire to pursue important immaterial truths. This is an abrogation to which I do not wish to contribute.

      These conclusions are contrary to my upbringing, and were ascertained alone. My upbringing was regretably material and secular. I was convinced of that worldview. It is incorrect.
